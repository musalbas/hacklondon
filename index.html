Frequently Asked Questions

What's up?

Watching cat videos on reddit. You?

Is it really free?

Yes.

Even food?

Yep. Thanks to our awesome sponsors. Drinks and frequent snacks are free too.

Wait, what's a hackathon?

A 24 hour long programming/building event. Students come together, form teams, share ideas, and help each other build awesome things. It's a fantastic opportunity to meet new people, learn from mentors, and make cool stuff. They're very beginner friendly.

Can I attend HackLondon?

Any enrolled undergraduate or postgraduate student, or graduates who are less than a year out of university. Students will be required to show their student ID upon arrival of the hackathon in order to enter.

Do I need to have a team?

You're welcome to make a team before the event, but loads of people come without a team. At the start of the event, we'll help everyone find a team. Your team members can be from other universities/schools. One of the best things about hackathons is meeting hackers from all over the country.

When is HackLondon?

28 February - 1 March 2015

What time should I arrive?

We'll let you know soon.

Who’s organising HackLondon?

KCL Tech, UCLU Tech, and UCL Entrepreneurs.

Can I build my hack before the event?

No. It's unfair and immediately disqualifies you from presenting or winning any prizes. You can think of ideas, just don't start building them. You can of course use libraries, frameworks, and templates.

For how long can I hack?

You'll have a straight 24 hours to hack. 12pm to 12pm. You're allowed to sleep (but hardcore hackathon hackers stay up), and we'll have some fun activities for everybody so you can catch a break.

What do I bring?

A laptop is pretty much essential. Some adventurous types bring desktops. Probably a phone charger, assorted cables and anything else for electronics you're bringing Warm and comfortable clothes, toiletries and a change of clothes are important too!

How large can teams be?

To be eligible for prizes, teams need to be 1-4 people. If you're okay not being eligible for prizes, you can totally have as many people on your team as you want.

Do you reimburse for transport?

Yes, MLH are able to reimburse attending hackers up to £40 for travellers outside of London. You can request reimbursement at the event. We love MLH.

Can I sleep at the venue?

Yes! We’ll do our best to make the venue really nice for you to crash. You are welcome to leave overnight to sleep in a real bed, but many hackers sleep over at the venue!

I can't write code but still want to attend. Help?

The hackathon community welcomes you with open arms - plenty of mentors want to help you make your first hack a reality. We'll have mentors on site to teach you lots of new skills and guide you towards creating your first hack. All the students are very friendly and willing to help each other out. Come along and be a part of the tens of thousands of hackers community worldwide.

Is there a code of conduct?

We follow MLH’s code of conduct. MLH is dedicated to providing a fun, harassment-free experience for everyone. We expect all of our attendees, sponsors, volunteers, and staff to be respectful and considerate of others. They are all required to agree with this code of conduct. Organisers will enforce it throughout the event.

Are there other hackathons I can go to?

Yes! There will be hackathons all over the country this Spring run by many different universities. MLH’s lineup of hackathons in Europe.
