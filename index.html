Frequently Asked Questions

When is HackLondon?

28 February - 1 March 2015

Who’s organising HackLondon?

KCL Tech, UCL TechSoc, and UCL Entrepreneurs.

Wait, what's a hackathon?

Hackathons are events where people compete to build interesting technical projects in under 24 hours. Students come together, form teams, share ideas, and help each other build awesome things. All skill levels are highly welcome, so don't be intimidated if you don't feel your coding is up to scratch.

Who can attend HackLondon?

Any enrolled undergraduate or postgraduate student, or graduates who are less than a year out of university. Upon arrival, hackers will be required to show their student ID card in order to be allowed entry.

Is it really free?

Yes.

Even food?

Yep. Thanks to our awesome sponsors. Drinks and frequent snacks are free too.

Do I need to have a team?

You're welcome to make a team before the event, but loads of people come without a team. At the start of the event, we'll be sure to help everyone find a team. Your team members can be from other universities/schools. One of the best things about hackathons is meeting hackers from all over the country.

What time should I arrive?

We'll let you know soon.

I can't write code but still want to attend. Help?

The hackathon community welcomes you with open arms - plenty of mentors want to help you make your first hack a reality. We'll have mentors on site to teach you lots of new skills and guide you towards creating your first hack. All the students are very friendly and willing to help each other out. Come along and be a part of the tens of thousands of hackers worldwide.

Can I build my hack before the event?

No. It's unfair and immediately disqualifies you from presenting or winning any prizes. You can think of ideas, just don't start building them. You can of course use libraries, frameworks, and templates.

For how long can I hack?

You'll have a straight 24 hours to hack, so that's 12pm to 12pm. Of course, you are allowed to sleep, but we find many people stay awake the whole time. We'll have some fun activities running throughout though, so you can catch a break often.

What do I bring?

A laptop is pretty much essential, but some adventurous people bring desktops. Also, don't forget the charging cables for whatever electronics you bring along! Warm and comfortable clothes, toiletries, and a change of clothes would be good too.

How large can teams be?

To be eligible for prizes, teams need to be 1-4 people. If you're okay not being eligible for prizes, you can have as many people on your team as you want.

Do you reimburse for transport?

Yes, MLH are able to reimburse attending hackers up to £40 for travellers outside of London. You can request reimbursement at the event. We love MLH.

Can I sleep at the venue?

Yes! We’ll do our best to make the venue really nice for you to crash. You are welcome to leave overnight to sleep in a real bed, but many hackers sleep over at the venue!

Is there a code of conduct?

Yes. We follow MLH’s code of conduct. MLH is dedicated to providing a fun, harassment-free experience for everyone. We expect all of our attendees, sponsors, volunteers, and staff to be respectful and considerate of others. We require all to agree with this code of conduct and organisers will enforce it throughout the event.

Are there other hackathons I can go to?

Yes! There will be hackathons all over the country this Spring run by many different universities. MLH’s lineup of hackathons in Europe.
